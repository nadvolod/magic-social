## Do this

- Open with one of these three proven frames only: **product/news announcement**, **personal build story**, or **tutorial promise**. Those are the only hook styles represented in the top cohort. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92]
- Make the post **scannable within 5 seconds** by using a **list or clearly segmented structure**; all three top references used list-like or sectioned formats, while several bottom posts used mixed narrative/problem formats. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92 vs. ref #90, ref #204]
- Lead with the **artifact or outcome**, not the implementation pain. The top posts foregrounded a launch, something built, or a tutorial; the weakest posts often foregrounded the problem itself. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92 vs. ref #90]
- Keep the CTA **lightweight and utility-aligned**: “details,” “link,” or “contact” beat overt promotion. The strongest references used low-friction CTAs, while a webinar/promo CTA appeared in a bottom post. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92 vs. ref #413]
- If you include numbers, make them **proof-oriented**, not just decorative. The strongest post paired numbers with a concrete comparison/feature announcement; bottom posts also had numbers, so numbers alone are not enough. [ref #95 vs. ref #90, ref #204, ref #413]
- Package technical content as a **repeatable lesson for many engineers**, not a niche setup detail for a few. Tutorial/build-story references outperformed posts focused on a specific config file or isolated workflow issue. [ref #94, ref #92 vs. ref #100, ref #90]

## Avoid this

- Avoid opening on a **localized engineering annoyance** unless you quickly elevate it to a widely useful pattern; the email-reminder implementation problem drew zero engagement. [ref #90]
- Avoid **promo-heavy packaging** in the body or CTA — links plus webinar framing plus product pitch correlated with bottom-cohort performance. [ref #413]
- Avoid relying on a **contrarian one-liner alone**; one bottom post had a sharp contrarian hook but still produced only 2 reactions, so the hook itself did not carry the post. [ref #204]
- Avoid making the core promise a **tool-specific config tweak**; even with decent impressions, that framing stayed in the bottom cohort on engagement. [ref #100]
- Avoid drawing lessons from incomplete examples; one bottom record has no usable text or signals, so it should not influence drafting decisions. [ref #93]

## How to exceed this cohort

The bar to clear is set by **ref #95** on reach and reaction volume, and by **ref #94/#92** on useful packaging for developer audiences. Your next post should be **more specific than the announcement post**, **more grounded in lived engineering experience than the tutorial post**, and **more credible than the build-story post by including concrete evidence, architecture choices, or before/after outcomes**. For this ICP, the winning move is likely not broader hype, but a sharper combination of **technical specificity + scannable structure + clear practical payoff**. The Raw Idea is non-negotiable subject matter — references inform tone and shape, not topic. If a reference pattern doesn't strengthen the Raw Idea, drop it.
