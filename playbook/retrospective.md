## Do this

- Open with a **plain-English news, build, or tutorial promise in the first line**; avoid making readers infer the payoff. This is consistent across the top references: announcement (#95), personal build intro (#94), and tutorial framing (#92). [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92]

- Use a **list-based or clearly segmented structure** for technical posts: either “intro → features/comparison → CTA” or a simple list of what was built/learned. All three top posts used highly scannable structures, while weaker posts were more narrative or mixed-format. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92, ref #90]

- Make the post about **something concrete that now exists**: a launch, a built app, or a tutorial. The top cohort is anchored in shipped artifacts, while several bottoms focus on process pain or configuration details. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92, ref #90, ref #100]

- If you include numbers, put them in service of **capability or comparison**, not just implementation detail. The strongest top post used numbers inside a launch/comparison frame, while bottom posts also had numbers but without the same broad reader payoff. [ref #95, ref #90, ref #204, ref #413]

- Keep the CTA **lightweight and utility-aligned**: “details,” “link,” or “contact” beat overt promotion in this sample. The top posts used soft CTAs, while a bottom post pushed a webinar/promo link. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92, ref #413]

- Prefer **educational or informative tone over promotional tone**. The top three are informative/educational; the clearest promotional example sits in the bottom cohort. [ref #95, ref #94, ref #92, ref #413]

## Avoid this

- Avoid leading with a **problem-only hook** when the solution/value is not immediately obvious; bottom posts #90 and #204 did this, while top posts led with the asset itself. [ref #90, ref #204, ref #95, ref #92]

- Avoid **promo-heavy CTAs** in the main post body; the webinar/promo framing in #413 underperformed relative to the softer CTAs in the top cohort. [ref #413, ref #95, ref #94, ref #92]

- Avoid making the post primarily about a **narrow tooling/config tweak** unless you can elevate it into a broader lesson or stronger outcome; #100 performed modestly despite solid structure. [ref #100]

- Avoid **mixed-format clutter** when a simpler list would do; top posts were structurally simple, while weaker posts combined narrative, bullets, code, bolding, links, and media. [ref #94, ref #92, ref #90, ref #413]

## How to exceed this cohort

The bar to clear is set mostly by **ref #95** on reach and by **ref #94/#92** on packaging technical work into a story or tutorial. Your next post should be **more specific than the top references**, **more grounded in lived engineering experience**, and **more credible with concrete outcomes or comparisons** than a generic launch/tutorial summary. It also needs a **clearer unexpected angle** than the bottom cohort’s problem-first posts: not just “here’s an issue,” but “here’s the artifact, why it matters, and what changed because of it.” The Raw Idea is non-negotiable subject matter — references inform tone and shape, not topic. If a reference pattern doesn't strengthen the Raw Idea, drop it.
