# Lessons Learned

### Posts must match the quality of good-social-posts examples

9 posts were rejected with "this doesn't match what we have in the good-social-posts folder at all." The example post has: a strong personal hook, a real problem discovered while building, actual code snippets, and a lesson backed by specific outcomes. Generic summaries of commit diffs do not meet this bar.

### No feedback within 3 days means the post wasn't interesting enough

16 posts received no feedback within 72 hours. If an engineer won't spend 10 seconds reacting to a post, that post has no value. Generate fewer, higher-quality posts rather than many mediocre ones.

### Every post must connect to AI agents, Temporal, or distributed systems

6 posts were rejected as "not_relevant." The target audience is AI engineers building distributed systems. Posts about generic coding practices, simple config changes, or unrelated topics waste everyone's time. Every post must have a clear connection to the niche.

### Stale posts indicate systemic quality failure

18 posts went stale (unpublished for 7+ days). This is not a feedback problem — it means the posts were not good enough to warrant any attention. When most posts go stale, the generation quality needs fundamental improvement, not more volume.
