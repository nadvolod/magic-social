# variant_2 — Three layers of what made Replay matter

**Intended audience:** Senior engineers and technical leaders building durable AI workflows who want signal, not hype, from conference recaps.
**Why it may perform:** The three-layer structure is easy to scan, mixes named people with named companies, and translates the event into a credible engineering takeaway without losing the human side.
**Risks:** Because it is structured and reflective, it may feel slightly more composed and less spontaneous than the most personal variant.

## LinkedIn-ready post (copy below)

```
Replay 2026 reminded me that durable AI is becoming a real engineering discipline.

And I got to feel that from three different angles.

First, the workshop layer.

I supported the Nexus workshop with Mason and the AI workshop led by Melissa as a teaching assistant.

That meant I was not just hearing ideas. I was watching engineers work through them in real time.

Second, the systems layer.

Some of the most interesting talks for me were from Netflix and OpenAI.

What stood out was not hype.
It was operational seriousness.

The conversation was about how Temporal helps scale AI operations when workflows have to be durable, recoverable, and production-safe.

That is the part of AI infrastructure I care about most.

Third, the human layer.

A conference can be technically strong and still feel forgettable.
Replay did not.

There was a secret Tiki room, live music, sonic gameplay, and glow-in-the-dark cotton candy.

But the real reason it felt memorable was the people.

You could feel 2000+ engineers trying to solve the same class of problems together.

Huge thanks to Temporal for hosting it, and especially to Mason, Melissa, and everyone I connected with there.

I left with a clearer picture of where durable distributed systems and AI orchestration are going.

Now I just want to get back to building.
```
