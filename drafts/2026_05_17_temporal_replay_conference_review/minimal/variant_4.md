Most engineering conferences get one of two things right:

The content.
Or the atmosphere.

Temporal Replay 2026 somehow got both.

On one side, you had serious conversations about durable execution, distributed systems, and AI operations.

On the other, you had a secret Tiki room, live music playing in front of giant retro game visuals, and glow-in-the-dark cotton candy.

That contrast sounds silly on paper.
But it captured something real about the event.

The future of AI infrastructure is being built by people who care deeply about correctness and reliability.
That doesn't make the work any less human.

I was lucky to support two workshops as a TA: the Nexus workshop led by Mason, and Melissa's AI workshop.

What I appreciated most was how grounded the conversations were.
Not hype.
Not vague "agents will change everything" claims.

Just engineers asking the production questions:

- how do we recover work after failure?
- how do we coordinate long-running AI tasks?
- how do we make AI operations observable and auditable?

Hearing companies like Netflix and OpenAI share how they're using Temporal at scale made that even more concrete.

Huge thanks to Temporal for hosting such a thoughtful event.

I left with new ideas, new connections, and a stronger sense that durable orchestration is going to matter a lot more over the next few years.

Also, every conference should consider adding glow-in-the-dark cotton candy.

What conference have you attended that actually got both the technical depth and the community feeling right?
