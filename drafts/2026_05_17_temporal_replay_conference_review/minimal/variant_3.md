I spent part of Temporal Replay 2026 helping as a TA in two workshops:

- the Nexus workshop led by Mason
- Melissa's AI workshop

That gave me a better signal than the keynotes.

You learn a lot about a technology by watching where engineers get stuck.

A few patterns kept showing up in the room:

1. Engineers are excited about AI agents.
But the hard questions start immediately after the demo works.

2. The real implementation pain is usually orchestration, not generation.
People ask about retries, state, resumability, timeouts, compensation, and observability far more than prompt wording.

3. Durable systems thinking is becoming an AI skill.
Not just a distributed systems skill.

That was the strongest theme I saw across Replay.

The talks from companies like Netflix and OpenAI reinforced it too. The interesting part wasn't "we use AI."
It was "here's how we run AI operations at scale without losing control of the system."

And honestly, I love that this is where the conversation is going.

We've had plenty of time in the industry focused on model magic.
Now more engineers are asking the production questions.

That's healthy.

Also, Replay had some of the best conference energy I've seen in a while: a secret Tiki room, live music with giant game visuals, and glow-in-the-dark cotton candy.

Huge thanks to Temporal, Mason, Melissa, and everyone who came through the workshops.

My biggest lesson from TAing:

**The future AI engineer probably looks a lot like a distributed systems engineer.**
