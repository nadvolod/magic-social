I went to Temporal Replay 2026 expecting to learn a lot about durable execution and AI orchestration.

I did.

But the part I'm leaving most grateful for is the people.

Replay was a beautiful gathering of 2,000+ engineers trying to build AI systems that are not just impressive, but dependable.

I got to support two workshops as a teaching assistant:

- the Nexus workshop led by Mason
- Melissa's AI session

There's something special about being in a room where people are asking real questions instead of performative ones.

Not "what's the hottest framework?"

More like:

- how do you recover long-running work?
- how do you make agents observable?
- how do you keep AI operations correct under failure?

Those are my favorite engineering conversations.

And outside the sessions, Replay somehow managed to feel both deeply technical and genuinely joyful.

A secret Tiki room.
Live music.
Glow-in-the-dark cotton candy.

That combination should not work.
It absolutely worked.

I also loved hearing how companies like Netflix and OpenAI are using Temporal to scale real AI operations. It's easy to talk about agents in the abstract. It's much better to hear how teams are actually running them.

Huge thanks to Temporal for hosting, and to Mason, Melissa, and everyone I met over the last few days.

I learned a lot.
But more importantly, I left energized by the kind of people building this space.

The future of AI infrastructure will be shaped by human beings who care about correctness, reliability, and each other.
